Ingredient Acquisition Difficulty (Food Distance Rankings)

Customize the "Acquisition Difficulty" for each ingredient. 1 represents items readily available in your pantry or kitchen (e.g. salt, garlic), while 10 represents items that are very hard to get (e.g. specialty meats, rare spices, long-distance shopping items). The recipe recommender computes the difficulty of missing ingredients to tell you which meals are easiest to achieve.

What does "Food Distance" mean?

For any recipe, we sum up the difficulty of all missing ingredients. This gives you a clear score of how much effort, distance, or expense it will take to make that meal.

Meal Distance = ∑ Difficulty of Missing Ingredients

Difficulty Reference Scale

  • 1-2 Ubiquitous / Staples: Always on hand, salt, water, basic spices, garlic.
  • 3-4 Common Groceries: Onions, eggs, rice, butter, milk. Easy grab.
  • 5-6 Fresh Proteins / Produce: Chicken, beef, fresh herbs, specific vegetables.
  • 7-8 Specialty Items: Imported seasonings, Gochujang, premium seafood, specific cheeses.
  • 9-10 Rare / Hard to Acquire: Organic direct-farm meats, rare ingredients, high cost items.
